  3. Finite differences on position

    4. In my first attempt, I tried to introduce an equilibration substep at each timestep in which I re-distributed mass between the Γ node and the C_0 node. However, I soon realized this is a bad approach. There is no physical process that equilibrates the interface with a slab of solution with thickness equal to the grid size.

    A more fruitful approach is to assert that C_0 is wholly slaved to Γ by the equilibrium relation, and that rather than redistributing mass between C_0 and Γ at each time step, Γ can be updated by a finite difference approximation of the flux onto the surface. I would like to make just one investigation: the sensitivity of the flux approximation to the number of nodes used in the finite difference calculation of the concentration derivative at the interface based on n nodes C_0, C_1, ... C_{n-1}.
